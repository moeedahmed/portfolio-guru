# UK Medical Portfolio Pathways — Research

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Status:** Research complete. Feeds Architecture decision: separate Portfolio Health (universal) from Pathway Guidance (stage-specific overlay).

## All Pathways — Summary Map

Every UK doctor needs evidence of competence. What differs is: who reviews it, on what schedule, against which standard, with what consequences.

| Pathway | Who | Review | Standard | Platform | Urgency | Evidence shape |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation (FY1/FY2)** | ~12,000/yr | Annual ARCP | FPCs (13 Foundation Professional Capabilities) | Horus (England), Turas (Scotland/NI/Wales) | High — exit gate to specialty training | SLEs, supervisor reports, learning log (30h core + 30h non-core), Form R, PSA |
| **Core Training (CT1-3/IMT1-3/ACCS)** | ~15,000 | Annual ARCP | Specialty curriculum + generic professional capabilities | Kaizen or specialty ePortfolio | High — determines ST3 entry | WPBAs, MSF, ESLEs, audit/QI, teaching, courses, exams |
| **Higher Specialty (ST3-8)** | ~25,000 | Annual ARCP | Specialty SLOs/KCs/CiPs | Kaizen (EM) or specialty platform | Medium-High — progressing to CCT | Same as core + management projects, leadership, research |
| **CESR / Portfolio Pathway** | ~87,000 non-training doctors (GMC 2025) | One-off evaluation (24-month window) | Knowledge, Skills & Experience vs curriculum high-level outcomes (CiPs) | Any platform; RCEM suggests Kaizen | High-anxiety — career gate to consultant posts | WPBAs (min 36 for RCEM: 12 DOPS, 12 Mini-CEX, 12 CBD), ESLEs, audit/QI, CPD, reflections, FRCEM (encouraged), structured reports, logbook |
| **SAS / Specialist Doctor grade** | ~15,000 | Annual appraisal + 5-year revalidation | GMC 6 domains + scope of practice | Any; some colleges offer ePortfolio | Low-urgency but sustained | CPD, QI, significant events, patient + colleague feedback, complaints, compliments, PDP |
| **Trust grade / Clinical Fellow / FY3+** | ~20,000 | Annual appraisal | No fixed framework; driven by GMC revalidation + future application scoring criteria | Self-managed | Medium — building toward training application | CPD, audit/QI, teaching, logbook, supervisor letters, courses, publications |
| **GP (trainees)** | ~6,000 | Annual ARCP | RCGP curriculum + WPBA requirements | FourteenFish / ePortfolio | High — CCT gate | COTs, CBDs, CEPS, QIP, MSF, PSQ, safeguarding, Form R |
| **GP (qualified, revalidation)** | ~45,000 | Annual appraisal + 5-year revalidation | GMC 6 domains + RCGP MAG framework | FourteenFish or equivalent | Low — maintenance | CPD, QI, SEAs, MSF, PSQ, complaints, compliments, PDP |
| **IMG pre-registration** | Variable | GMC registration assessment | PLAB/UKMLA + English language + evidence of qualifications | GMC portal | High — entry gate | Degree, internship evidence, exam results, English test |

## Pathway Deep Dives

### 1. Foundation Programme (FY1–FY2)

**System:** Horus ePortfolio (England), Turas (devolved nations)

**What they file:**
- SLEs (no fixed minimum, ~5–10 per 4-month placement is typical)
- Supervisor end-of-placement reports
- 30 hours core learning log + 30 hours non-core
- Form R (probity/health declarations)
- PSA (Prescribing Safety Assessment) by end of F1

**Curriculum:** 13 Foundation Professional Capabilities (FPCs), not SLOs/KCs. FPCs 1–5 require direct observation.

**ARCP outcome:** Determines progression to FY2 or completion. Exit gate controls specialty training eligibility.

**Portfolio Guru relevance:** Low for EM-specific filing. Foundation portfolio is simpler and Horus is adequate. However: FY2s applying to EM training are building toward portfolio scoring for recruitment — they need publications, teaching, QI, and specialty experience that Horus doesn't track well.

### 2. Core / Higher Specialty Training (CT1–ST8)

**System:** Usually Kaizen EP (EM, Emergency Medicine) or specialty-specific ePortfolio

**RCEM curriculum (EM-specific):** 12 SLOs with KCs. 2025 update (6 August 2025) added new KCs to SLO 5 (paediatric resuscitation), SLO 6 (procedural skills/sedation), and SLO 12 (leadership/management).

**ARCP requirements (RCEM per year):**
- ESLE: min 3 (1 in each placement, PEM-focused where applicable)
- MSF: min 1 (first 6 months of year)
- ESR: 1 per year
- CSR: 1 per placement (PEM + Adult if not with ES)
- QIAT: min 1 per year (covers SLO11 + SLO12)
- Management portfolio: 4 mandatory assignments ST3–6 (rota, risk/clinical governance, guideline/policy, complaint/critical incident)
- Procedural log: all procedures
- CBD/DOPS: RCEM emphasises quality over quantity — once signed off independent, no further DOPS needed

**ARCP structure:** Annual, with defined calendar dates per deanery. Deterministic outcome (1–6). Failure can mean extended training or removal.

**Portfolio Guru fit:** Primary current audience. Kaizen filing, SLO/KC mapping, form recommendations all target this.

### 3. CESR / Portfolio Pathway (Non-Training → Specialist Register)

**GMC name change:** CESR → "Portfolio Pathway" (30 November 2023). Old CESR label still widely used.

**Standard:** Moved from "equivalence to CCT" → "Knowledge, Skills & Experience (KSE) for practicing as a consultant in the UK." Maps to curriculum high-level outcomes (CiPs/SLOs), not individual KCs.

**RCEM-specific requirements:**
- Min 36 WPBAs: 12 DOPS + 12 Mini-CEX + 12 CBDs
- ESLEs focused on core specialties
- CPD and reflections
- FRCEM encouraged but not mandatory
- WPBAs within last 5 years preferred
- Specialist medical qualification + ≥6 months specialist training in the specialty
- 24-month window once GMC application opened
- Processing: 3–6 months after submission
- Structured reports from consultants

**Key difference vs ARCP trainees:** No annual review. No training programme coordinator. Self-directed, no deadlines. The anxiety is: "Is this even the right evidence? Am I on the right track?" rather than "Did I file enough by the deadline?"

**Platform:** Not tied to Kaizen. Can use any platform. RCEM provides a logbook template and a "Template of Competence vs Evidence" spreadsheet.

**Portfolio Guru fit:** High. The WPBA drafting engine works generically. The health tracking needs CESR-specific curriculum mapping (GMC SSG + RCEM SLOs/CiPs, not training-stage specific KCs). No Kaizen dependency needed for health layer.

### 4. SAS / Specialist Doctor Grade

**Who:** ~15,000 UK doctors. Includes: Specialty Doctor, Associate Specialist, Staff Grade, LAS, trust doctors on SAS contracts.

**Career progression options:**
- Remain at current grade (annual appraisal + 5-year revalidation)
- Apply for Specialist Doctor grade (senior SAS role, autonomous practice, leadership/teaching/research opportunities)
- Portfolio Pathway → GMC Specialist Register → Consultant
- Apply to training programme (if eligible)

**Evidence requirements (revalidation):**
- CPD (GMC: minimum 50 hours/year)
- Quality improvement activity (audit, case review, service improvement)
- Significant events / serious incidents + reflection
- Patient/service-user feedback (once per cycle)
- Colleague feedback / MSF (once per cycle)
- Complaints and compliments (annual review)
- PDP (reviewed vs previous year)
- Scope and nature of work statement

**SAS-specific portfolio to specialist registration:**
- More detailed evidence than revalidation alone
- Must show consultant-level competence across full specialty breadth
- Evidence can be UK or overseas, must be current (within 5 years)
- Specialty-specific guidance from relevant College
- Supported by multiple consultant reports

**Portfolio Guru fit:** Medium. WPBA drafting engine works for the clinical evidence. The health tracking needs the GMC 6-domain framework rather than RCEM SLOs. This audience doesn't use Kaizen heavily — standalone health tracking is more valuable than Kaizen filing.

### 5. Trust Grade / Clinical Fellow / FY3+

**Who:** ~20,000 doctors between training stages or outside formal training. Labels overlap: trust grade, clinical fellow, LAS, locum consultant (non-substantive).

**No unified framework exists.** Evidence collected serves two purposes:
1. GMC revalidation (annual appraisal, 5-year cycle)
2. Future specialty training applications (scored against person specification domains)

**Common evidence collected:**
- Clinical: logbook, WPBAs, supervisor reports, procedures
- Academic: publications, presentations, posters, research
- Teaching: formal teaching, feedback, course organisation
- QI/Audit: completed cycles, presented, implemented changes
- Leadership: rota management, guideline development, committee membership
- Courses: ALS/ATLS/APLS, specialty-specific courses, degrees/diplomas

**Key pain point:** No one tells them what to collect or how to structure it. They discover requirements retrospectively when applying to training.

**Portfolio Guru fit:** Very high. This is the most underserved group. They need:
- "What should I be collecting for EM training applications?"
- Domain coverage against person specification scoring criteria
- Draft WPBA-style entries from clinical cases
- Gap analysis without an ARCP deadline

### 6. GP (Trainees)

**System:** RCGP ePortfolio (often via FourteenFish)

**WPBA requirements (2025):**
- CBDs + COTs (Consultation Observation Tools) per training period
- At least 1 audio COT + 1 face-to-face COT
- CEPS (Clinical Examination & Procedural Skills) — all intimate exams by ST3 end
- QIP (1 per year, must be completed in GP post)
- MSF + PSQ (Patient Satisfaction Questionnaire)
- Safeguarding evidence + knowledge update
- Form R annually

**Curriculum:** 2025 refreshed curriculum, same assessment structure.

**Portfolio Guru fit:** Low for now. GP has its own ecosystem (FourteenFish is £49.99/yr and purpose-built). WPBA drafting would need GP-specific form types and language. Not in RCEM/Kaizen ecosystem. Roadmap: revisit after trainee EM PMF.

### 7. GP (Qualified, Revalidation)

**System:** Annual appraisal + 5-year revalidation cycle

**Platform:** FourteenFish dominates (~£49.99/yr). Features: mobile CPD capture, email-to-portfolio, built-in PSQ/MSF surveys, offline sync, organisation management.

**Evidence:** GMC 6 supporting information categories + RCGP MAG framework. CPD, QI, significant events, feedback (patient + colleague), complaints/compliments, PDP.

**Portfolio Guru fit:** Minimal. FourteenFish has GP appraisal locked. EM/acute specialties are the gap.

### 8. IMG / Pre-Registration

**Who:** Doctors with overseas qualifications seeking GMC registration.

**Routes:** PLAB/UKMLA → GMC registration → can then enter training or non-training pathways. Some specialties have "approved postgraduate qualification" routes (MRCP, MRCS, etc.).

**Portfolio needs:** English language evidence, degree proof, internship evidence, exam results, clinical attachments/observerships for CV building. Portfolio Guru offers little here — this is a registration gate, not evidence collection.

## Common Thread — What Every Pathway Shares

Regardless of stage, every UK doctor needs evidence organised around:

1. **Clinical competence** (WPBAs, logbook, cases, procedures)
2. **Professional development** (CPD, courses, conferences, exams)
3. **Quality improvement** (audit, QI projects, service improvement)
4. **Teaching** (formal teaching, feedback, course delivery)
5. **Leadership & management** (committees, guidelines, rota, governance)
6. **Reflection** (on cases, feedback, incidents, learning)

The only difference is:
- **Which framework** they're mapped to (RCEM SLOs vs GMC domains vs person specification scoring)
- **What cadence** they're reviewed on (annual ARCP vs one-off CESR vs 5-year revalidation)
- **What urgency** they carry (CCT deadline vs self-directed vs maintenance)

## Implications for Portfolio Guru Architecture

**Portfolio Health** (universal layer) should track:
- Evidence inventory by type, date, and domain
- Domain coverage (clinical, teaching, QI, leadership, etc.)
- Filing cadence (regular vs burst)
- Gap identification (thin domains)
- Evidence status (drafted, filed, reviewed, accepted)
- Platform agnostic — works with or without Kaizen

**Pathway Guidance** (plugs into Health) — selectable overlays:
- **Training / ARCP** → maps against RCEM SLOs/KCs + training stage requirements + ARCP count rules
- **CESR / Portfolio Pathway** → maps against GMC SSG + RCEM CiPs/SLOs + 36-WPBA requirement
- **SAS / Career grade** → maps against GMC 6-domain revalidation framework
- **Trust grade / Application prep** → maps against specialty person specification scoring domains
- **GP** → future, if EM PMF succeeds
- **Foundation** → low priority, Horus is adequate

**The architecture decision:** Build Portfolio Health first as the universal evidence tracker. The pathway layer sits on top and interprets the same evidence inventory differently depending on selected pathway — it's a view, not a separate data model. This means one user can switch pathway views without re-entering evidence.

**Building the pathway selector now** (pre-build) matters because:
1. The `/health` chart already exists but is hardcoded to RCEM KCs
2. Refactoring it later means migrating user data
3. The non-training audience is 3x larger than trainees
